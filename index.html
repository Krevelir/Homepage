Education and Career

I am a final year student of English Philology at Nicolaus Copernicus University in Toruń, with a specialization in translation. I love videogames, so I wanted to work with them in whatever year I can. After two internships, where I worked with business and legal texts, I decided to start translating games. I played a part in translating PAYDAY 2 , content for Minecraft released in 2022, and a few other most recent Microsoft games. I do not think I can ever beat these achievements in my career as a translator, so I decided to add programming to my list of competences. In October 2022, I took a front-end developer course at YouCode programming school, which is a really effective and enjoyable experience.

About Me

I got interested in programming years ago, but only recently have I began to approach the matter with proper seriousness. I decided to learn the front-end side of projects, as it allows me to use my creativity and immediately see the result of my work. In a way, I feel both like a mechanic and an artist, making sure that everything works and looks the way it is supposed to.

🔧 Few activities have captivated my attention as much as fixing issues with my PC, both on the hardware and software level. I would often forget about the passing of time while following numerous tutorials on how to reduce sudden noise coming from my computer, trying different ways to force an uncooperative machine to launch, looking for one faulty file that prevents my favorite game from working properly — These processes would often take entire days, but I never complained and always found a solution.

🏰 I've always liked to create things especially castles — sand castles, Minecraft castles, castle drawings on notebook margins. It is most likely due to my love for historical movies or fantasy worlds like The Lord of the Rings or The Elder Scrolls, which have an abundance of fortifications. So, why is is relevant for a programmer? Well, the best castles need to:

Be solid — every segment, no matter if it is made of wood, stone or magical metal, needs to be sturdy enoigh to withstand a siege.
Be practical — each element has a strategic value and needs to be accessible to defenders and incaccessible to the attackers.
Be visually appealing— a castle needs to look appropriate for its setting, have its own atmosphere and take into account wonders of nature, be it a hill or a river.
Be meaningful — real or fictional castles that stay in our memories have one thing in common — they carry a story. Great action sequences, triumphant victories or devastating defeats make a fortress stand out.

The same rules apply to programming. A good product is well-coded, has useful functions, looks appealing and helps people get what they are looking for. If a problem-solving activity combined with building castles can be a job, then that is exactly the job I want.
